BMI (Body Mass Index) is calculated by dividing your weight (kg) by your height in meters squared.

Formula: weight(kg) ÷ (height(m) × height(m))

Ranges:
< 18.5 = Underweight
18.5–24.9 = Normal
25–29.9 = Overweight
≥ 30 = Obese

Note: BMI does not differentiate between muscle and fat — a muscular person may have a high BMI but low body fat.
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) represents the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic functions while at rest.

Use Case: It's the foundation for calculating your Total Daily Calorie Needs (BMR × activity level).
Most online calculators use approximation formulas (like the US Navy or YMCA method). They are not as precise as professional tests like DEXA scans.

Tip: Use the same calculator consistently to track progress over time rather than focusing on the absolute number.
Protein needs vary depending on activity level and goal:
  • Inactive / Sedentary: 0.8 g per kg body weight
  • Active / Strength Training: 1.4–2.0 g per kg
  • Fat Loss with Muscle Preservation: 1.6–2.2 g per kg
Consult a healthcare professional if you have a medical condition.
